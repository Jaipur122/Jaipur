Traditional Clothing

Before visiting Jaipur, the nordic city of warm and supper hot Indian. You should know more about the look behind the traditional clothes that millions of Jaipur's people wear both as a symbol of their country but also as good protection from the hot sun. The beginning of the traditional, royal clothes of Jaipur started when Maharaja Jai Singh II founded and decided to make the Jaipur that is also called the Pink city a place with special clothes designed as a representation of the royal era.

Different cloths for men and women

Something that represents the traditional men's clothes is, the dhoti kurta Rajput poshak and a special place for a sword on the waist side. That was originally a traditional way to represent the royalty behind Rajasthani men. Even women have a special way of dressing themself to show and represent their tradition, religion and a good way to protect the skin and hair. Ghagra Choli, the original cloth that was designed for women, is a long blouse with a long skirt and a long dupatta on the head. Together they look like a long dress that often is made of very light and colorful fabric that represents the Indian color palette.

Festival and traditional dances

Indian and especially Jaipur is well known for their traditional Indian dances and festivals. Is a very religious city with a lot of Tempel and sacred construction that is very special for religious and traditional reasons. A big part of the tradition is about feasts, dances, food, and most important festivals. During the years there are a lot of big festivals with their own historical and cultural reason for celebration. One of the biggest is Teej is one of the most important festivals in the whole of India and especially in Jaipur. It is an integral part of Hindu mythology that they find very important and fun to celebrate. Originally the festival was for women to celebrate and welcome mother nature and pray to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati.

Traditional handicraft of Jaipur

Jaipur street markers and shops are known for extraordinary handicraft that represents both Indian and Jaiours culture and the traditional side of the city. Jaipur's nature resorts are full of different rare and expensive metals, materials, and even plants and animals. Therefore it is imperative to know more about some accessories and beautiful and traditional Jaipuri handicrafts that you can buy all over the city.

  • Lac Bangles: These traditional bangles were originally made of wood but are now decorated with a lot of other materials such as metals, diamonds, and beautiful stones.
  • Puppets: Traditional Indian puppets that are used as home decor and different tv shows and theater games.
  • Gems and Metal Jewelry: This is a special design for making beautiful jewelry from gold, silver, diamonds, and other metals.
  • Blue Pottery: One of the most traditional handicrafts in Jaipur. It was specially made, painted, and designed pots with stunning symbols that are painted in clear blue color.
